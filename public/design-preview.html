Foundation

Design Principles

Four principles guide every visual and interaction decision across all WorshipBuddy products. When in doubt, return here.

01 — Unified
One system, many surfaces
Every element contributes to a cohesive whole. No isolated features, no one-off styles. Design at scale — a component that works on the homepage should work in the app, in the docs, and in email. Consistency is trust.
worshipbuddy churchbuddy presenterbuddy
02 — Universal
For every church, everywhere
WorshipBuddy serves a global, diverse community. Color contrast passes WCAG AA. Type stays legible at small sizes. Layouts work in RTL. Interactions work without a mouse. By meeting accessibility standards we respect all users.
WCAG AA RTL-ready Keyboard nav
03 — Iconic
Unmistakable at a glance
WorshipBuddy products should be instantly recognizable. The Instrument Serif × JetBrains Mono typographic contrast, the per-product color identity, and the flat-color discipline create a visual signature no competitor has. Function and distinctiveness aren't in tension.
WorshipBuddy WorshipBuddy
04 — Conversational
Motion that explains itself
Animation exists to communicate, not decorate. Transitions tell users where they are and where they went. Hover states confirm interactivity. Entrance animations reveal hierarchy. Every motion should have a reason a user could articulate — even if they never would.

Section Transitions

Inspired by instrument.com — sections flow into each other rather than abruptly cutting. Use sequential background rhythm to guide the eye down the page.

Background Sequence (homepage)
1. Hero + WhoWeAre
Warm White · #FAFAF9
2. Product Showcase
Surface · #F4F4F0
3. Stats Counter
Dark · #0F172A
4. Support Us
Warm White · #FAFAF9
5. CTA Banner + Footer
Dark · #0F172A
Rhythm rule: Alternate between light (#FAFAF9 / #F4F4F0) and dark (#0F172A) sections. Never place two dark sections back-to-back unless intentionally grouped (e.g. CTA + Footer). The contrast between surface temperatures creates the page flow instrument.com uses.

Rules

Do & Don't

✓ Do
Use flat color for all buttons and CTAs. btn-wb, btn-cb, btn-pb are the only colored buttons. Solid fills, full opacity.
✕ Don't
Use gradient fills on buttons. background: linear-gradient(...) on any interactive element ages badly and conflicts with the flat-color discipline.
✓ Do
Use Instrument Serif for section headings and hero text. The serif + mono contrast IS the visual identity. Even one section headline in serif lands the brand.
✕ Don't
Use Inter, Poppins, or system-ui as a deliberate choice. These make WB look like every other SaaS tool. The font system is a key differentiator.
✓ Do
Use product colors purposefully — only on that product's own UI, buttons, badges, and active states. Blue = WorshipBuddy. Amber = ChurchBuddy. Violet = PresenterBuddy.
✕ Don't
Mix product colors freely or use them as generic accent colors. If a section is about all three products, use the neutral ink color, not any one product color.
✓ Do
Use rounded-full only on pills, badges, and the nav CTA button. Cards, sections, inputs, and modals use the tiered radius system (4–16px).
✕ Don't
Use rounded-full on cards, images, or section backgrounds. Pill-shaped cards look playful and conflict with the precision/software aesthetic.
✓ Do
Alternate section backgrounds: warm white → surface → dark → warm white → dark. This creates the visual rhythm that guides users down the page.
✕ Don't
Use custom background colors (blue sections, colored hero backgrounds) unless for a specific product page. The neutral palette is the canvas — product colors are the accents.
✓ Do
Use JetBrains Mono for product names in nav, eyebrow labels, version numbers, key stats, and footer column headers. Lowercase product names in mono look intentional.
✕ Don't
Use JetBrains Mono for body copy, long-form descriptions, or any text over 14px in normal reading contexts. Mono is an accent — it loses its power when overused.
Rules

Voice & Copy

WorshipBuddy sounds like a team that built serious software and genuinely cares about worship ministry. Not pastoral. Not corporate. Not startup-hype.

Tone qualities
  • →
    Precise: Specific numbers, named features, honest limitations. "247 songs" not "thousands."
  • →
    Direct: "Free forever — no credit card, no catch." Not "Start your free journey today."
  • →
    Warm: We are worship leaders too. The mission is genuine — let that show in copy.
  • →
    Confident: Don't hedge. "WorshipBuddy is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit." Not "WorshipBuddy aims to be..."
✓ Write this
"Everything your worship team needs" · "No ads. No purchases. Just worship." · "Free forever — no credit card, no catch."
✕ Not this
"Empowering worship communities with innovative solutions" · "Transform your worship experience today!" · "The leading platform for ministry teams"
📖
The mission lives in the copy, not the UI chrome
WorshipBuddy looks like serious software — that's intentional. The spiritual warmth and nonprofit mission communicate through words, not pastel backgrounds or cross icons. Trust the content. Let the design be clean.
